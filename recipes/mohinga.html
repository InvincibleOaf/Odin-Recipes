Mohinga Recipe

Description

Mohinga is a classic Burmese dish. It is often eaten for breakfast in Burma/Myanmar, but many people enjoy it for lunch or dinner. The dish primarily consists of fish, onions, and garlic cooked with noodles - and the pi√®ce de resistance is ngapi - that is, rotted shrimp paste. It's more delicious than it sounds - trust me!

Ingredients

Serves 3-4

  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, finely diced/crushed
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, finely grated
  • 2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tin pilchards in tomato sauce, spines removed
  • salt, to taste
  • cooking oil
  • gram(chickpea) flour
  • 1 Tbsp ngapi - or shrimp paste or fish sauce, if unavailable
  • Vermicelli rice noodles
  • Boiled eggs (optional)

Steps

  1. Add onion and garlic and add to a large saucepan. Cook in oil until translucent but not brown.
  2. Add ginger, turmeric and pilchards. Add ngapi/shrimp paste/fish sauce. Stir gently and keep on low heat
  3. Make a paste using gram flour and water. Approx 1 heaped tsp of gram flour to 500mls water. Add the water slowly to create a smooth paste, ensuring there are no lumps. Gradually add this mixture to the pan with a spoon.
  4. Taste the Mohinga and add anything that feels missing - salt, garlic, gram flour, water etc.
  5. Cook noodles, add to dish and enjoy!
